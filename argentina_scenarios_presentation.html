Conclusion · phased action plan

Phased rollout — scale to 9 now, return to 7 / 9 vehicles in 60 days

Absorb BsAs fleet today · structural cleanup once production is steady
Phase 1 — Now (0–60 days)
Scale to 9 active drivers · absorb new BsAs vehicles
  • Hire 2 new drivers (region flexible — start by picking up the new vehicles in Buenos Aires, then deploy wherever production needs)
  • UNBLOCK FIRST: rescue Matías's vehicle from the shop and expedite Mauro's documents by direct courier — both have been idle through admin/mech issues, not performance
  • Resolve Fernando's and Leandro's blockers (Cédula verde and lidar) in parallel
  • Authorize hotel mode for Guillermo on longer trips (PMO recommendation)
  • Open expense audit on Matías's reimbursements (PMO concern)
  • Keep all 7 current drivers active + 2 new hires → 9 drivers total to clear the BsAs inventory backlog
  • Conduct formal performance review with Mauricio during the window, documenting PMO concerns
Phase 2 — Rebalance (60+ days)
Return to 7 drivers / 9 vehicles
  • Once BsAs fleet is absorbed and production stable, downsize to 7 drivers / 9 vehicles (original target ratio)
  • Separate Mauricio with structured offboarding — supported by Phase-1 performance review and PMO documentation
  • Separate one additional driver — final pick decided by Phase-1 data (productivity, idle days post-unblock, area fit)
  • Redistribute their vehicles across the remaining 7 drivers based on best geographic coverage
  • If Phase-1 review of Mauricio is favorable, defer the decision and re-evaluate the second cut only
Why phased this way: The new BsAs fleet creates short-term demand that the current 7 can't absorb without overload — hiring 2 buys capacity to clear the backlog while keeping options open. The 60-day window doubles as a clean evaluation period: with admin/mech blockers removed, idle-day data becomes a fair signal of performance. By Phase 2, the second-cut decision rests on real productivity data, not pre-unblock noise — and Argentine labor law (Ley 20.744) requires this documented basis to manage dismissal risk.
